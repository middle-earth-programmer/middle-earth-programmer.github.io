Understanding the Electoral Formula

Costa Rica uses a unique, proportional representation system based on the Hare Quota system with a secondary threshold requirement. Seats are allocated across provinces based on three primary terms:

1

The Quotient (Cociente)

The total valid provincial votes divided by the number of seats available in that province.

2

The Subquotient (Subcociente)

Half of the Quotient. Parties must receive at least this many votes in a province to even qualify for the seat allocation process.

3

Greatest Remainder (Residuo Mayor)

Leftover seats that are not distributed cleanly by the full Quotient are given sequentially to the qualifying parties with the largest leftover vote remainders.
